# Shirt School CTAs

## Primary CTAs (use in most videos)
- Subscribe for more shirt business tips
- Check the description for links
- Comment below: [question directly related to video topic]
- Hit the notification bell so you don't miss new videos

## Secondary CTAs
- Watch this video next: [related topic]
- Download the free [resource] in the description
- Join the Shirt School community

## End Screen CTAs
- "Watch this next" — point to the most relevant related video
- Subscribe reminder — keep it brief and warm

## Sponsorship CTAs
- Check out [sponsor] — link in the description
- Use code SHIRTSCHOOL for [discount]
- I've been using [product] for [X months/years] — genuinely recommend it

## Community CTAs
- Share this with someone who's starting a shirt business
- Tag us in your shirt business wins @shirtschool

---

## What NOT to Say
- "Smash that like button" — feels forced, not Kerry's style
- "Drop a comment down below" — "below" is stiff
- Long multi-step CTAs — pick ONE per video, keep it clean
- "If you found this helpful, consider subscribing" — too passive and hedged
- Excessive urgency ("do this RIGHT NOW before it's too late")

---

## Notes on Kerry's CTA Style
- Keep CTAs natural and conversational — integrated into the content, not bolted on
- The subscribe ask should feel like a genuine recommendation, not a demand
- Pair CTAs with specific value: "Subscribe so you don't miss when I release the pricing breakdown video"
- CTA questions in comments should be actionable and specific, not generic ("Drop your best tip below!")
- For sponsorships: only promote things Kerry actually uses and believes in
- CTA timing: mention once mid-video (if at all) and once at the end — not at the top
